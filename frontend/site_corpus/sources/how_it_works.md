# How does Teivaka work?

TFOS operates through three foundational layers:

- tasks,
- events,
- and auditability.

Once these three ideas are understood, the rest of the platform becomes clear.

## 1. Tasks are the operational interface

Farmers interact with TFOS primarily through guided operational tasks, not through dashboards or complex menus.

Tasks are generated from multiple operational signals, including:

- crop schedules,
- livestock cycles,
- weather conditions,
- compliance windows,
- delivery timing,
- ferry schedules,
- and farm activity history.

The farmer does not need to understand the underlying operational logic. They see the next recommended action, complete it, and record the outcome.

This creates a low-friction operational workflow designed for real farming environments.

## 2. Every activity becomes a structured event

Farm activity is recorded as structured operational events:

- planting,
- spraying,
- harvesting,
- labour activity,
- input purchases,
- deliveries,
- and payments.

Each event is anchored to:

- the farm,
- the block,
- the production activity,
- and the responsible operator.

Events form the foundational data layer of TFOS.

Reports, recommendations, analytics, compliance records, forecasting, and operational summaries are all generated from the event history.

Nothing inside TFOS exists without a traceable operational event underneath it. Reported outcomes can always be linked back to specific recorded activities over time.

## 3. Auditability creates operational trust

TFOS maintains tamper-evident audit records for operational events.

Each event receives a unique fingerprint linked chronologically to prior records, creating a verifiable chain of operational history. Altering a past record breaks the chain visibly.

This allows third parties — buyers, extension officers, lenders, processors, agricultural programs — to review operational history with greater confidence in record consistency and integrity.

TFOS does not rely on memory-based farming records. It creates structured operational evidence.

## Operational Example

A farmer opens TFOS in the morning and receives a recommended task based on current farm conditions and operational schedules.

Once completed, the activity is recorded as an event with associated operational details such as:

- crop,
- block,
- operator,
- timing,
- and related farm activity.

Over time, these events accumulate into a structured operational history that can support:

- farm management,
- reporting,
- compliance,
- delivery verification,
- and future financing assessments.

This is the complete operational pattern. Every other capability inside TFOS is a refinement of these three layers.
