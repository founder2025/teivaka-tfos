# Founder Statement

I left a Science Degree and returned home to Kadavu to farm.

In one season, I learned something every Pacific farmer already carries but few systems recognise:

Pacific farmers produce real economic value inside systems that rarely record, measure, or properly recognise the work behind it.

For generations, many farms across the Pacific have operated through memory:

- planting dates remembered mentally,
- yields estimated,
- labour untracked,
- input use undocumented,
- and operational history carried verbally rather than structurally.

That approach was never a failure of the farmer.

It was the absence of infrastructure built for the realities farmers actually operate within.

The result is that a farmer can spend twenty years producing crops, feeding markets, and managing land — yet still appear institutionally invisible when assessed by lenders, buyers, insurers, or any formal system that requires structured evidence.

The land is not the problem.
The farmer is not the problem.

The problem is the absence of systems capable of translating agricultural work into trusted operational proof.

TFOS was built to solve that problem.

Not from a theory.
Not from a lab.
Not from a conference room.

It was built on working farms first, under real production conditions, before asking any other farmer to trust the platform with their own season.

Teivaka exists to help transform undocumented agricultural activity into structured operational history — creating systems that respect the work Pacific farmers have always done, but which institutions have historically struggled to see.

— Uraia Koroi Kama
Founder, Teivaka PTE LTD
